Standard corrections

The two corrections routinely performed on TA datasets:

  • pretrigger offset compensation (POC)
  • background correction (BGC)

The pretrigger offset compensation subtracts the average of the pretrigger part of every time trace from the whole time trace and therefore corrects for DC offsets in the data that are frequently encountered.

The background subtraction is a basic background correction that takes the first few time traces, averages over those, and subtracts the average from every time trace of the dataset, therefore correcting for the laser-induced background that is visible in off-resonant signal parts.

Whereas the former can be applied without damage or side effect to every dataset, the same is not necessarily true for the background subtraction.

Advanced corrections (dialogues)

For more advanced "corrections", two separate GUI windows are available:

  • baseline correction (BLC)
  • accumulation (ACC)

Both GUI windows come with their separate online help.

Smoothing (running average filter)

To reduce the noise level, it is often convenient to smooth data with a running average filter with a given filter window.

Currently, only "boxcar" is available, being a non-weighted running average filter that averages over the given filter window width.

Be aware of that filtering with a window too large compared to the spectral features can easily lead to distortions of the spectra. Therefore, use with care.

MFE

Permanently sum MFon and MFoff data together in the MFoff trace. Used e.g. for background traces that were recorded with both field-on and field-off traces to improve S/N.

Applying this operation to a dataset will lead to the dataset only having MFoff data.

Adding and subtracting one dataset from the other

Please note: Currently, this feature is still very experimental and only tested for 1D datasets. Applying it to 2D datasets may lead to unpredictable effects and most probably errors. Only the Apply works currently. Therefore, try to work always with copies of your datasets.
